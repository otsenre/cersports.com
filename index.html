Under The Hood

How The Model Works

Three independent engines feed a consensus layer every morning. The output is a single CCR score per game — we post the ones above the threshold.

STEP 01
Data Scrape
Live game logs, rest days, back-to-backs, team ratings, and injury context pulled fresh from Basketball Reference every morning via Playwright.
STEP 02
Rule Engine
Upgraded rule-based model scores every matchup — net rating, pace, shot profile, fatigue index, and recent form all weighted and combined.
STEP 03
ELO + XGBoost
A trained XGBoost model on a decade of game data produces win probability and predicted margin for every game on the day's slate.
STEP 04
Live Odds
Current market lines pulled via The Odds API. Spread and ML edge calculated in real time against our model's projected margins.
STEP 05
Consensus Pick
All three model outputs cross-referenced. Agreement scoring produces a unified CCR rating. Only plays above threshold qualify for publication.
STEP 06
Publish
HTML report generated. Social cards rendered. CCR-branded captions written. Published daily to YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, and X.
Our Proprietary Rating

What is CCR?

The CerSports Confidence Rating is a 0–100 score assigned to every pick by our consensus model. It measures how much edge our system has detected across all three models combined.

  • CCR 90+ — All models in full agreement. Elite edge. Rare, and worth noting when it hits.
  • CCR 80–89 — Strong multi-model consensus. Our standard qualifier for posted picks.
  • CCR 68–79 — Solid data lean. Worth tracking but below our posting threshold.
  • Below 68 — Noise. We don't post what the model doesn't believe in.
